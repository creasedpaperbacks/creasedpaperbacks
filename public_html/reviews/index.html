21 Lessons for the 21st Century

This is a classic airport book. Yuval Noah Harari opines on 21 current developments, ranging from mediation to fake news to the future of work. These are fascinating and highly topical issues, and I’ve copied some of his thoughts here. On work In the past, machines competed with humans mainly in raw physical abilities, while humans retained an immense edge over machines in cognition. Hence as manual jobs in agriculture and industry were automated, new service jobs emerged that required the kind of cognitive skills only humans possessed: learning, analysing, communicating and above all understanding human emotions.

The Pioneers by David McCullough

This book follows the leaders of the Ohio Company of Associates and their settlement into the Ohio territory. Manasseh Cutler was to be the spokesman for the “Ohio cause” on the scene with the Congress in New York. The word “lobbyist” had yet to come into use. Rather he was the “agent” assigned to win congressional approval, and no fitter or more capable agent could have been selected.

Maybe You Should Talk to Someone by Lori Gottlieb

This book describes various therapy sessions as seen through the eyes of a practitioner. Lori’s train-of-thought, running commentary about her conversations with patients provides colour into their lived experiences and makes us empathise with their struggles. She interweaves into the storyline her own experiences and her own conversations with her therapist, creating a rich, autobiographical tapestry which reminds us how emotionally frail each of us can be. Quotes We talk to ourselves more than we’ll talk to any other person over the course of our lives but our words aren’t always kind or true or helpful—or even respectful.

Becoming by Michelle Obama

The first part of Michelle Obama’s autobiography is particularly worth reading. We all know Michelle as a (former) First Lady, but not so much about her life before that. This first section of the book candidly describes her upbringing in a working-class neighbourhood. “I’ve been lucky enough now in my life to meet all sorts of extraordinary and accomplished people—world leaders, inventors, musicians, astronauts, athletes, professors, entrepreneurs, artists and writers, pioneering doctors and researchers.
